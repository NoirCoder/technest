## Design & Build Quality

The MX Master 3S feels like what a $100 mouse should feel like. The soft-touch coating, metal scroll wheel, and weight distribution scream "premium" from the moment you pick it up.

### Ergonomics That Actually Work

Let's talk about the shape. This isn't your standard office mouse. The MX Master 3S has a pronounced thumb rest and an angled body that naturally guides your hand into a relaxed position. After 8-hour workdays, I had zero hand fatigue—something I can't say about the generic mouse I used before.

The 141-gram weight might seem heavy on paper, but in practice, it adds to the quality feel without making long sessions tiring.

### Premium Materials

The shell uses a combination of soft-touch plastic and textured rubber on the thumb rest. It's grippy without being sticky, and after three weeks, I haven't seen any shine or wear on the coating.

The metal scroll wheel is incredibly satisfying. It's electromagnetic (more on that later), so there's no physical clicking mechanism to wear out or break.

## Features & Specifications

Here's what you're getting:

- **Sensor**: Darkfield laser, 8000 DPI (adjustable)
- **Battery**: Rechargeable Li-Po, 70 days per charge
- **Connectivity**: Bluetooth or Logitech Bolt USB receiver (included)
- **Buttons**: 7 programmable buttons
- **Multi-device**: Connect up to 3 devices
- **Weight**: 141g
- **Dimensions**: 124.9 x 84.3 x 51mm

### The Standout Features

**MagSpeed Scroll Wheel**: This is the headline feature, and it's genuinely game-changing. Switch between precision ratcheted scrolling (for fine control) and free-spin mode (for blasting through long documents) with a single click. The electromagnetic mechanism is silent, smooth, and feels like magic.

**Gesture Button**: The side thumb button can be customized for gestures—I use it to switch between virtual desktops, but you can set it to anything via Logitech Options+ software.

**Multi-Device Switching**: Press a button on the bottom to switch between 3 paired devices instantly. I use it between my work laptop, personal desktop, and iPad.

## Performance

I tested the MX Master 3S across different scenarios: spreadsheet work, photo editing, video scrubbing, and general web browsing.

### Daily Productivity

For spreadsheet work (where precision matters), the 8000 DPI sensor tracks perfectly on any surface—including glass, thanks to Logitech's Darkfield technology. I never had to adjust sensitivity or fight with cursor jumping.

### Creative Work

In Photoshop and Premiere Pro, the scroll wheel's free-spin mode excels. Scrolling through 500+ layers or a 30-minute timeline becomes effortless. The side scroll wheel (yes, there's a second wheel for your thumb) handles horizontal scrolling, which is clutch for wide timelines.

### Battery Life

Logitech claims 70 days per charge. After three weeks of 8-hour workdays, the battery is still at 82%. The USB-C charging is fast—about 3 hours for a full charge, or 3 minutes for a full day of use.

### Software

The Logitech Options+ software (free) lets you customize every button and create app-specific profiles. For example, my browser buttons differ from my Photoshop buttons. It's intuitive and works on both Windows and Mac.
